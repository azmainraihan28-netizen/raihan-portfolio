## The Problem

1. Professionals waste 30–45 minutes every morning clicking through Twitter, Reddit, and Google News — mostly noise
2. Context switching before 9 AM destroys deep work windows for the rest of the day
3. Generic newsletters (Morning Brew, etc.) cover topics irrelevant to niche professionals like automation experts
4. Important industry news (funding rounds, tool launches, competitor moves) is missed for days with no proactive monitoring
5. Manual "read later" curation systems always collapse — nobody maintains them consistently

## The Solution

A scheduled n8n workflow (9 nodes) that:
- Triggers at 7 AM Mon–Fri via cron schedule
- Pulls articles from 3 RSS feeds (TechCrunch, The Verge, VentureBeat — swap any URLs)
- Deduplicates and normalizes all articles into a clean list
- Sends the batch to GPT-4o-mini: generates top story + analysis, 4–6 quick hits, 1 AI insight, 1 action item
- Builds a beautiful branded HTML email and delivers it automatically
- Cost: ~$0.001 per digest (less than 1 cent/day)

## How It Works

Node-by-node flow:

- **Schedule Trigger** — Mon–Fri 7 AM
- **Set (Config)** — recipient email, name, topics, max articles, OpenAI key
- **RSS Feed Read (×3)** — TechCrunch + The Verge + VentureBeat
- **Merge** — Combines all 3 feed streams
- **Code** — Deduplicates by title, normalizes fields
- **HTTP Request (OpenAI)** — GPT-4o-mini JSON mode: digest generation
- **Code** — Builds full branded HTML email
- **Gmail** — Sends digest to recipient
