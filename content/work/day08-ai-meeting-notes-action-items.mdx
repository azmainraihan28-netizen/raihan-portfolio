## The Problem

1. 50–70% of meeting action items are never completed — they get lost in messy notes
2. Writing and distributing meeting summaries wastes 15–30 minutes after every call
3. Manually creating tasks in Asana/Trello from meeting notes is tedious and error-prone
4. Attendees forget context by the time they see the task — leading to poor execution
5. People who miss the meeting have no quick way to get up to speed

## The Solution

A webhook-triggered workflow that:
- Accepts raw meeting transcript (from Zoom, Fireflies, Otter, or manual paste)
- Uses GPT-4o-mini to extract every action item with assignee, due date, priority, and context
- Creates tasks/cards directly in Asana OR Trello (single toggle to switch)
- Posts a meeting summary + key decisions to Slack automatically
- Returns a structured JSON response for front-end or Zapier integration

## How It Works

Node-by-node flow:

- **Webhook** — Receives meeting transcript (POST)
- **Set** — Normalizes and extracts fields
- **IF** — Guards against empty transcripts
- **OpenAI (gpt-4o-mini)** — Structured action item extraction
- **Code** — Safely parses AI JSON, handles edge cases
- **Split Out** — Loops over each action item individually
- **IF** — Routes to Asana vs. Trello based on payload flag
- **Asana** — Creates tasks with notes, due date, assignee
- **Trello** — Creates cards with description, due date, priority label
- **Aggregate** — Collects all created tasks before notification
- **Slack** — Posts meeting summary + error alerts
- **Respond to Webhook** — Returns confirmation JSON (×2)
